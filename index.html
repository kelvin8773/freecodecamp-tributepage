Andrew Yang (Entrepreneur)

The man who want to help millions Americans in Next Industrial Revolution!
Andrew Yang is an American entrepreneur, the founder of Venture for America, and a 2020 Democratic presidential candidate.

    Here's a time line of Mr.Yang's life:

  • 1975 - born in Schenectady, New York
  • 1992 - He graduated Exeter in Phillips Exeter Academy, and went on to attend Brown University earning a Bachelor's of Arts (BA) in Economics. After Brown University, Yang attended Columbia Law School in New York City where he earned a Juris Doctor.
  • 1999 - He graduated from Columbia Law School, Yang began his career as a corporate attorney at Davis Polk & Wardwell.
  • 2000 - He left the firm to launch Stargiving.com, a startup that worked to support celebrity-affiliated philanthropy. Stargiving.com raised capital from investors but later folded in 2001.
  • 2001 - Afterwards, Yang joined a healthcare software startup MMF Systems, Inc., as its Vice President and third hire.
  • 2005 - Yang left MMF Systems, Inc. to join friend Zeke Vanderhoek at a small test preparation company, Manhattan Prep.
  • 2006 - Vanderhoek asked Yang to take over as CEO. While serving as CEO of Manhattan Prep, the company primarily served GMAT test preparation becoming the largest U.S. test prep company reaching $18 million in annual revenue. The company expanded from 5 to 27 locations and was eventually acquired by Kaplan, Inc. in December 2009, at which point Yang served as the company's President through the year 2011.
  • 2011 - Yang founded his new company - Venture of America, a non-profit, with the mission "to create economic opportunity in American cities by mobilizing the next generation of entrepreneurs and equipping them with the skills and resources they need to create jobs."
  • 2017 - Yang stepped down as CEO of the company, and filed with the Federal Election Commission (FEC) to run for President of the United States in 2020. He is running on the campaign slogan "Humanity First", which calls attention to his belief that automation of many key industries is one of the biggest threats facing the workforce.
  • Today - Yang is the first Asian American man to run for President of the United States as a Democrat. The campaign has been considered a long shot. If elected, he would be the first Asian American President of the United State.
